While the term “March Madness” needs no introduction, its origin story is worth telling. The now-iconic phrase, used to describe the 68-team NCAA men’s basketball tournament held every March, traces its roots back to Huff Hall in 1939.
Illinois high school athletics administrator H.V. Porter coined the phrase in a newsletter, inspired by the electrifying atmosphere at Huff Hall during state basketball tournaments.
From the chaos of high school championships to today’s bracket-fueled frenzy, it all started here: https://storied.illinois.edu/march-madness/
