March Madness 2026: Tournament Format & Structure

March Madness 2026 follows the traditional 68-team single-elimination format, creating an unpredictable and action-packed tournament where every game matters. With the best college basketball programs competing alongside Cinderella teams looking to make history, the road to the national championship is filled with drama, upsets, and unforgettable moments. Here’s a breakdown of how the tournament is structured.

Selection Process & Seeding

The tournament field is announced on Selection Sunday, where the NCAA Selection Committee reveals the 68 teams competing in March Madness.

  • 32 teams receive automatic bids by winning their conference tournaments.
  • 36 at-large teams are selected based on their regular-season performances, strength of schedule, and advanced metrics.
  • Each team is placed in one of four regions (East, West, South, Midwest) and seeded from 1 to 16 within their region.
  • The final four at-large teams and the four lowest-seeded automatic qualifiers compete in the First Four for a spot in the main bracket.

First Four

The tournament kicks off with the First Four, where eight teams fight for four spots in the main 64-team bracket.

  • Dates: March 17-18, 2026
  • Location: UD Arena – Dayton, OH
  • Format: Four games featuring the lowest-ranked at-large bids and lowest-ranked automatic qualifiers.

First & Second Rounds

The madness begins as 64 teams compete in back-to-back rounds over the first weekend of the tournament.

  • First Round (64 teams): March 19-20, 2026
  • Second Round (32 teams): March 21-22, 2026
  • Single-elimination: Win or go home.
  • Potential upsets: Lower-seeded teams often take down higher seeds, creating Cinderella stories.

Sweet 16 & Elite Eight

The remaining 16 teams battle for a spot in the Final Four, facing tougher competition and higher stakes.

  • Sweet 16 (16 teams): March 26-27, 2026
  • Elite Eight (8 teams): March 28-29, 2026
  • Each regional champion advances to the Final Four.

Final Four & National Championship

The Final Four determines the national champion in front of a packed crowd and millions of viewers worldwide.

  • Final Four (4 teams): April 4, 2026
  • National Championship (2 teams): April 6, 2026
  • Location: Lucas Oil Stadium – Indianapolis, IN
  • Winners of each semifinal face off for the title.

March Madness 2026: Top Teams & Players to Watch

March Madness 2026 is shaping up to be another unforgettable tournament, featuring powerhouse programs, rising underdogs, and future NBA stars. Top Contenders for the National Championship

These powerhouse programs have dominated the regular season and enter the tournament as top seeds, with their sights set on cutting down the nets in Indianapolis.

  • Kansas Jayhawks – Led by a veteran squad and one of the best coaches in the game, Kansas boasts a dominant inside presence and elite guard play. They’ve been battle-tested in the Big 12 and are primed for a deep run.
  • Duke Blue Devils – With a mix of five-star freshmen and experienced upperclassmen, Duke remains a perennial title threat. Their high-octane offense and elite perimeter defense make them a nightmare matchup.
  • Purdue Boilermakers – After last year’s shocking early exit, Purdue is back with a vengeance. With their dominant frontcourt and sharp-shooting backcourt, they could finally break through to win it all.
  • Arizona Wildcats – The Wildcats play at one of the fastest paces in the nation and have multiple NBA-caliber players. If they get hot, they’re capable of torching any defense in the field.
  • Connecticut Huskies – The defending champions are hungry to repeat, relying on stifling defense and clutch scoring. UConn knows how to win in March, making them a dangerous opponent for anyone.

Dark Horse Teams That Could Shock the Field

Every year, a lower-seeded team captures the nation’s attention by making an unexpected deep run. Here are a few potential Cinderellas in 2026.

  • Creighton Bluejays – A well-coached, disciplined team that plays efficient basketball. If their three-point shooting catches fire, they could go deep.
  • Florida Atlantic Owls – Coming off recent tournament success, FAU is no longer a secret. Their experienced roster and up-tempo offense could cause major headaches for higher seeds.
  • Dayton Flyers – Backed by a rowdy home crowd in the First Four, Dayton has a history of pulling off upsets. Their balanced attack makes them a team to watch.
